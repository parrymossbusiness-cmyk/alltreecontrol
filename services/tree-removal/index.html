Tree Removal Services in Little Rock, AR

Safe, complete removal for dead, hazardous, or unwanted trees — from a single backyard oak to full lot clearing, with 24/7 emergency response.

When a tree needs to come down in the Little Rock metro, the job is rarely as simple as cutting through the trunk. Central Arkansas properties are dense with mature oaks, pines, and sweetgums growing close to houses, fences, driveways, and power lines, which means most removals require careful rigging, directional felling, or crane assistance to protect everything around the tree. All Tree Control removes trees of every size, from storm-weakened limbs threatening a roofline to full acres of overgrown lot that need clearing before construction begins.

Every removal starts with a free, on-site assessment. An arborist looks at the tree's lean, root condition, proximity to structures, and access for equipment, then gives you a written price before any chainsaw starts. We carry full liability and workers' compensation coverage, so you're protected if anything doesn't go exactly to plan — though with our crews, that's rare. Once the estimate is approved, we schedule the work, bring in a wood chipper and hauling truck, and leave the property cleaner than we found it, with all debris removed unless you'd like the wood left for firewood.

Hazardous Tree Removal

A hazardous tree is one that poses an active risk to people or property before it ever falls — think a split trunk, a severe lean over a driveway, or root damage from recent construction or flooding. These trees need a trained eye to assess correctly, because the danger isn't always obvious from the ground. Our arborists check for internal decay, girdling roots, and structural cracks, then plan a removal sequence that controls exactly where each section falls. In tight yards, that often means climbing and lowering pieces by rope rather than felling the whole tree at once. We also flag hazardous trees during routine visits for other services, so if we spot a problem while grinding a nearby stump or pruning a neighboring tree, we'll let you know before it becomes an emergency call. Insurance companies and HOAs in the Little Rock metro frequently require documentation of hazard removal, and we can provide a written assessment on request.

Lot & Land Clearing

Building a new home, adding a driveway, or opening up a wooded lot in Saline or Faulkner County often means clearing more than one or two trees. Our land clearing crews handle everything from selective thinning that preserves a handful of specimen trees to full clearing of an acre or more ahead of a construction crew. We work with builders and homeowners directly, coordinating timelines so the site is ready when grading and foundation work begin. Because central Arkansas soil holds a lot of clay, we pay close attention to root removal and grading so cleared land doesn't leave hidden stumps or uneven ground behind. Debris can be chipped on-site, hauled away, or burned where local ordinance allows — we'll walk you through the options that fit your property and budget during the estimate.

Crane-Assisted Tree Removal

Some trees simply can't come down safely with rigging and rope alone — a massive trunk overhanging a house, a tree wedged between two structures, or a removal where ground crews have no safe drop zone. In those cases, we bring in a crane to lift sections directly off the tree and set them down in a controlled location, eliminating the guesswork of a directional fall. Crane-assisted removal costs more than a standard felling job because of the equipment and setup involved, but it's often the only responsible option when a tree sits within a few feet of a roofline, pool, or neighboring property line. We coordinate street permits and utility clearances when a crane needs to set up in a right-of-way, so the logistics are handled before our crew ever arrives on-site.
